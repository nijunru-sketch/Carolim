# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary viewers are HR teams and CEOs reviewing the site as part of hiring decisions. They need a fast, credible read on experience, portfolio quality, and whether the candidate can ship tasteful AI-assisted work.

## Product Purpose

This is a personal hiring site that presents past experience, selected work, and proof of technical and visual craft. Success means the viewer quickly understands the candidate's background, sees strong project evidence, and leaves with confidence in both coding ability and taste.

## Brand Personality

Clean, calm, design-aware, confident. The voice should feel considered rather than loud, with enough breathing room and restraint to suggest editorial polish.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic template portfolios, overly flashy motion, crowded "everything everywhere" sections, and anything that feels like a stock SaaS landing page.

## Design Principles

- Make the first impression feel intentional and lightweight.
- Let typography, spacing, and motion do most of the work.
- Prioritize clarity of experience and work over self-promotion.
- Use motion as a reveal, not as decoration.
- Keep the system modular so later pages can be added without rethinking the whole structure.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Respect reduced-motion preferences, keep text legible and contrast-safe, and ensure the site reads cleanly on desktop and mobile for quick screening.

## Collaboration Workflow

This project is coordinated through one dispatcher session and multiple visible page sessions.

- The dispatcher session keeps the shared direction, resolves conflicts, and merges finalized page work.
- Each visible session owns one page or one tightly scoped page family.
- Frozen surfaces stay fixed unless the dispatcher explicitly asks for a change.
- New pages should be started in a new visible session instead of being hidden inside an assistant-side subagent.
- When a page reaches draft-final or final status, that session should hand back the exact files it changed, what was verified, and any open risks.
- Sessions should not edit files owned by another page unless the dispatcher has reassigned that work.
